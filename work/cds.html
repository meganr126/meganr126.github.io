What I've done with The Daily Sun.

I am an Assistant Design Editor on The Cornell Daily Sun, the country's oldest independent college daily newspaper.

About

I joined The Cornell Daily Sun Design Department during the Spring 2016 semester. I made staff at the end of that semester and was promoted to senior staff during the Fall 2016 semester. I ran for and was elected Assistant Design Editor in March of 2017, less than a year after making staff; the usual time between joining a department and joining the editorial board is over a year and a half.

My first project as editor was to create a page checking process. This helped standardize print paper designs and ensure that quality is maintained throughout the late-night editing process. The system has been a great success. Now, I am working with the Board of Directors to help the Sun transition to Adobe inDesign.

This coming year, the inDesign transition will require a lot of work from the Design Department. We will be training the rest of the editorial board and newspaper staff as well as taking of the role of the production manager. From when we receive a story to when the printer receives our files, the entire process will be lead by the Design Editor team.
